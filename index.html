Travel Gallery

Blue Lagoon

Blue Lagoon is one of the most popular tourist attraction throughtout Iceland. It is a geothermal heated spa located about an hour away from Reykjavik. You do not want to miss this! Relaxing in this heated mily water felt very rejuvenating when I went there right after I graduated! The start contrast of cold air and semi-hot mineral-filled water felt amazing beyond words.

Northern Lights

You may have seen it in pictures but not in person. The Northern Lights are colored lights that appear in the polar regions during night. Think of it as a natural fireworks when solar wind blows electrons into Earth's atmosphere and mix gases. Mixing gases is what makes particles glow. You will have the best chance of seeing it during the winter months.

Diamond Beach

Unlike other beaches in the world, the Diamond Beach takes the cake. Sorry ladies, the crystal-like structures that are laying by the shores are not actually giant diamonds. Nor is this beach one of the beaches that you would want to go swimming in. What you are seeing are chunks of icebergs that broke off from Jökulsárlón Glaciers that floated ashore. The black sands have originated from volcanic materials that have grinded into fine particles over time.

Shanghai Tower

Located in Shanghai, the purplish tower stands at 50 meters tall and constructed with eleven steel spheres in all sizes. This crown jewel attracts tourists from all over the world and guess how long the line is? On busy summer season, it takes FOUR hours just to get in and another THREE hours to get out! But it was worth the wait! Inside the middle sphere, I stood a glass floor that gives you the panoramic view including looking straight down!

Children from Nicaragua

Back in June 2011, I decided to go on a medical mission trip in Nicaragua right before I started my freshmen year in college. I volunteered as a pharmacy technician helping organize medicines and deliver to patients who are living below the poverty line. The children in this picture are grateful that they got the treatment that they needed the most.

Summer Palace

Located in Beijing, this former royal imperial gardeen was built in 1750 by Emperor Qianlong to celebrate his mother's birthday. Emperors and royal family members spent their free time strolling around this marvelous garden. It is now one of the biggest tourist attraction site that attracts tourists all over the world.

Eiffel Tower

As you may know, Eiffel Tower is located in Paris, France. It is one of the most famous tourist attraction in the world. It was originally called the 300-meter tower back in the day when it was first built. The tower has been built for the world fair in 1889 and over 2 million people have attended it. Now, the tower has also become the crown jewel and national pride for France. The tower may look great during the day but it looks breathtaking during nighttime! It would also be one of the most popular for men to propose to their beloved fiance.

Mount Jungfraujoch

Mount Jungfraujoch is located between three towns that cover parts in Switzerland: Lauterbrunnen, Grindelwald, and Interlaken. The name literally means "maiden saddle". It peaks at 4,158 meters above the sea level. At that level, it is the highest peak throughout Europe! You will get a very breathtaking view once you reach the top! Be careful not to run. Walk up slowly. The air is considerably thinner than at the ground level. But guess what? At 4,158 meters, that's only about 40% of getting to top of Mount Everest! It's not even halfway. Can you imagine what kind of endurance it will take to become a great mountainer climber?

Giza Pyramids

As you may have seen before, the Giza Pyramids are located in the western city of the Nile River called the Giza. The pyramids have been built around 2500 BC. The workers have toiled all day to build them as a tomb for generations for pharoahs. Unlike the popular myth, the workers were not slaves. They were normal citizens working for the government just like how we have USPS workers. Imagine how hot it was for them to be working outside all day! The first day I arrived in Egypt, the temperature reached staggering 40 degrees Celsius! But the view of the pyramids has been definitely worth it! This is one of the last surviving Seven Wonders of the World today.

The Colosseum

I don't know if you have watched the movie The Gladiator but it was based on the ancient stadium, located in Rome. It was built around 70 AD during the height of the Roman Empire. Emperors and other Roman citizens enjoyed gladiators fighting, dramas, and public executions. The Colosseum seated more than 50,000 so multiple entrances have been constructed as well. It may now sit in ruins but millions of tourists go there every year. When you visit Italy, Colosseum is not something you would want to miss.

Venice

Traveling to Venice would not be complete unless you ride in one of the gondolas and listen to Carnival of Venice or other romantic violin pieces. It would be like going to Paris but not seeing Eiffel Tower. Venice has been used as a port town for trading during the Medieval Period. Another interesting fact about it is that it is sinking between one to two millimeter per year. Rumors has it that Venice could be partially submerged by 2030. There is one crazy rule that I have learned while I travelled there. You are not allowed to sit down in public unless you're in a cafef or restaurant. If you get caught, then you either have to pay the fine on the spot or spend a night in jail.

Danube River

Located in capital of Austria, Vienna, this particular river has been an inspiration to this world-renowned composer Johann Strauss. Even if you have never heard of his name, I still think you may have heard of his most famous waltz, The Blue Danube Waltz. I heard that he may have taken a canoe ride along the river one time and got inspired. When I arrived in Vienna, I do admit that the river was gorgeous. I am a big lover of music. I came to Vienna alone to feel the essence of composers such as Mozart, Beethoven, and Strauss. Not only did I go sightseeing, the best part for me in Vienna was that during my last night, I attended the small concert in Kursalon. The orchestra was much smaller than in traditional symphony centers. There was not even a conductor waving his wand! The musicians were very well-synchronized. Out of everything I've seen, I enjoyed the dancing couple the most. The ballerina looked so gorgeous! She twirled and kicked high gracefully! Back to the river, I definitely want to visit Vienna again in the future.
